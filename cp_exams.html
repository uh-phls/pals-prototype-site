Comprehensive Exam - Ph. D. in Counseling Psychology

Comprehensive Exam Qualifications

  • Completion of 36 hours of coursework
  • Successfully defended candidacy paper at least 10 days prior to the exam date
    • Completion of 9 of the 18 hours required for the COE Doctoral Core. 3 hours must be in research.
    • Completion of at least 15 hours in the major of study
    • Completion of all requirements of candidacy research paper, including submission of paperwork.
    • Approved degree plan on file.
    • Approved application for full-time doctoral residency is on file.

Students are notified in writing of approval to take the exam.  The EPSY Examination Committee also notifies students in writing indicating the room in which the exam is administered.  Students should contact their advisors if they do not receive these letters within two weeks of the exam date. Comprehensive exams are offered in the fall and spring semesters.  

Components

The exam consists of two components:

  1. General Knowledge (3) hours

Students write for three hours about:

  • History and professional issues in Counseling Psychology
  • Pertinent theory
  • Research 

All students testing on the same day receive the same question. This portion is evaluated anonymously by at least two faculty members in Counseling Psychology.

  1. Specialty Question (6 hours)

An individualized question relating to one of the following areas of emphasis:

  • Supervision and Training
  • Career and Vocational Counseling
  • Consultation and Intervention
  • Theory, Process, and Outcomes of Counseling and Therapy

This portion is evaluated by at least two faculty members familiar with the student's research and/or specialty interest.  The two portions are graded independently, but the student must pass both portions in order to pass the exam.

Notification of Exam Results

The Examination Committee will notify the faculty and the Academic Affairs Office of the examination results.  Advisors are responsible for communicating exam results to their students. Students also receive official notification of the results from the College.

If a student fails one portion of the exam (General or Specialty), only that portion must be retaken.  The faculty has the option of giving an oral exam but only in those cases in which they cannot make a clear decision based on the student's written response.

Remediation Procedures for Students Who Fail

When a student fails the comprehensive exam:

  • A remediation committee is formed, consisting of two faculty members, not including the advisor.
  • The committee reviews the student's performance on the exam, as well as the student's academic and professional history
  • The committee devises an educational plan, the student and advisor review it and the committee considers their suggestions before it is finalized.
  • The student may not retake the exam until the plan is completed, as determined by the committee
